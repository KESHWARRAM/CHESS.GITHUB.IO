Piece Movement

‚ôô Pawn

  • Moves forward 1 square (2 squares on first move)

Special Rules

Castling

  • King moves 2 squares toward rook; rook moves to opposite side
  • Only if:
    • King has never moved
    • Rook has never moved
    • No pieces between king and rook
    • King is not in check
    • King doesn't pass through check
  • Two types: Kingside (O-O) and Queenside (O-O-O)

En Passant

  • Pawn capture on opponent's double-push advance
  • Only possible immediately after opponent's 2-square pawn move

Pawn Promotion

  • Pawn reaching opposite end must promote
  • Choose: Queen, Rook, Bishop, or Knight
  • Queen is usually strongest choice

Win & Draw Conditions

Checkmate

  • King is in check AND cannot escape
  • Checking player wins

Stalemate

  • King is NOT in check but has no legal moves
  • Game is a draw

Insufficient Material

  • Neither player has enough pieces to checkmate
  • Game is a draw (e.g., King vs King)

Resignation

  • Click "New Game" to start a new game at any time

Common Questions

Can I take back a move?

No. Once played, moves are final. Start a new game with "New Game" button.

Why can't I move my king there?

The king cannot move into check (where it would be attacked). It's an illegal move.

What if my king is in check?

You MUST immediately move your king out of check or block/capture the attacker.

How long does the computer take?

Easy moves instantly. Medium takes 1‚Äì3 seconds. Hard may take 5+ seconds depending on position.

Why is a move blocked?

All illegal moves are prevented by the game engine. The move violates chess rules or leaves your king in check.
